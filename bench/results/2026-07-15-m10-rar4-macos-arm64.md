# RAR4 (M10): real-corpus CBR decode

RAR4 method-29 cannot be authored by rar 7.x, so its performance is
measured on the owner's real CBR corpus via the conformance runner
(`KONI_ARCHIVE_CORPUS_DIR=… dart test koni_archive/test/conformance/`),
which decodes every page and verifies its SHA-256 against `unrar`.

- date: 2026-07-15
- dart: 3.12.2 (stable) on macos_arm64 (Apple Silicon)
- os: macOS 26.2 (Build 25C56)
- baseline: `unrar` 7.23 (ground truth; times below are koni_archive)

| corpus archive | method | pages | decode + verify (wall) |
| --- | --- | --- | --- |
| 7SEEDS v01.cbr | RAR4 store (-m0) | 181 | ~0.3 s |
| MPD Psycho v01.cbr | RAR4 method-29 (-m5) | 179 | ~1.8 s |
| Twin Spica 01.cbr | RAR4 method-29 (-m3) | 184 (183 + 1 filtered) | ~4 s |

## Reading the numbers

- The flagship, "CBR (v4) works", is met on real files: every page
  decodes **byte-identical to unrar** (SHA-256 verified). The one exception
  is a single filtered double-page spread in Twin Spica (RarVM, deferred),
  which surfaces as a per-entry typed error while the other 183 pages
  verify.
- Wall time includes reading each page from disk, the clean-room
  LZSS+Huffman decode, and a full CRC-32 verify. A comic reader opens one
  page at a time, so per-page latency (a few ms to ~20 ms for large
  method-29 pages) is what matters, well within a page-flip budget.
- The decoder is correctness-first (matches unrar), not yet
  micro-optimized; a flat Huffman lookup table already handles the hot path.
